Module 3: Two-Tier Cloud & Shadow Masking

Why Two-Tier Masking?

Simple spectral thresholds fail to identify dark cloud shadows or classify bright concrete features correctly. Training an Attention U-Net with the spectral guess enables the network to learn spatial contexts, mapping both diffuse cloud borders and adjacent shadows while ignoring urban structures.

Module 6: Judges Cross-Examination Defense Guide

Interactive guide explaining and defending Team Dhruva's architectural choices against targeted scientific questions from the ISRO panel.

Panel Question 1

"Why go through the complexity of a Cross-Attention mechanism? Why not simply concatenate the SAR bands as extra channels to your input layer?"

Defense: Concatenating SAR channels directly into the input layer forces the convolutional filters to process optical and radar intensities uniformly. Because radar has completely different scattering dynamics (VV/VH roughness returns vs. visible solar reflectance), this uniform blending leads to geometric artifacts, structural blurring, and radiometric shifts in clear-sky regions. By decoupling them and using a Cross-Attention loop, we restrict the SAR inputs to act purely as structural keys under cloudy pixels ($M=1$), leaving clear-sky pixels ($M=0$) completely untouched and radiometrically pure.

Channel Concatenation (Competitors)

Mixed statistics -> Blurring, radiometric drift, geometric artifacts.

Decoupled Attention (Team Dhruva)

Clear pixels are pristine. Cloudy pixels borrow structures from SAR.

Panel Question 2

"LISS-IV imagery features incredibly sharp details. Won't a generative diffusion process blur those fine spatial features?"

Defense: We deploy two defense mechanisms. First, we use a Restormer backbone with Gated Convolution Feed-Forward Networks (GDFN), which operate along the channel dimension while using multi-dilation depthwise convolutions to retain high-frequency spatial structures. Second, our optimization objective enforces a joint loss comprising Spectral Angle Mapper (SAM) loss and Multi-Scale SSIM. The MS-SSIM loss penalizes structural deviations at multiple scales, mapping the high-frequency radar boundaries directly to the reconstructed bands, ensuring LISS-IV sharpness is maintained.
